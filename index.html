1. Bayes Classification Rule 1.1 Decision Rule Let \(\delta(\mathbf{x}) \rightarrow \left\{0, 1 \right\}\) be the classification rule for class 0 or 1. Research: Using linear mixed-effects model to detect fraudulent erasures at an aggregate level

2019-03-10
My Research
Abstract Wollack and Eckerly (2017) extended the ‘erasure detection index’ (EDI) to detect fraudulent erasures at the group level. Sinharay (2018) suggested two modifications of the EDI at the group level. Those EDIs were not developed specifically for the fraudulent schools with small number of examinees with erasures. This paper suggested a new modification of the group-level EDI in Wollack and Eckerly (2017) by incorporating the ‘empirical best linear unbiased predictor’ (EBLUP) from the linear mixed-effects model.
